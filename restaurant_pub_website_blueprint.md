**Website Blueprint**

**Restaurant / Pub**

Final implementation brief for a high-converting, locally focused hospitality website.

> **Use this document as a handoff to your designer, developer, copywriter, or SEO lead.**
> It sets the site structure, page requirements, geo/SEO priorities, content needs, design rules, technical standards, and launch checklist.

| Prepared for           | Website planning and build handoff                                                  |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Recommended site type  | Hybrid: one strong scrolling homepage plus a small set of focused internal pages    |
| Primary business goals | Bookings, walk-ins, menu views, private hire enquiries, and local search visibility |
| SEO focus              | One main town/city + a few genuine nearby modifiers; no thin area pages             |
| Date                   | 22 March 2026                                                                       |

> **Core recommendation**
> Do not build a pure one-page site. Build a small multi-page site with a homepage that feels simple, scrollable, and conversion-led.

# 1. Executive summary

This blueprint is designed for a single-location restaurant, pub, or gastropub website that needs to convert local visitors into bookings, orders, enquiries, and walk-ins. It assumes the venue wants a modern, credible web presence without overbuilding the site.

The website should feel simple to the visitor, but it should not be a pure one-page site. The best structure is a compact multi-page website built around a strong homepage and a few high-intent internal pages.

> **The model to build**
> Homepage + Menu + Book a Table + Events / Private Hire + Find Us. Add About only if the brand story materially helps trust or pricing power.

## Non-negotiable decisions

| **Decision**                 | **Why it matters**                                                     | **Instruction**                                                                    |
|------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Hybrid site, not single page | Supports SEO, dedicated user intent, and easier navigation             | Use separate URLs for menu, booking, events/private hire, and location             |
| Geo-led local SEO            | Hospitality search demand is strongly location based                   | Optimise the homepage around the main town/city and use nearby modifiers naturally |
| Mobile-first UX              | Most visitors check hours, menu, directions, and booking on mobile     | Design for quick tap targets, fast loading, and sticky CTAs                        |
| Real HTML menu               | Better usability, accessibility, and indexability than a PDF-only menu | Use live text for the menu page; keep PDF optional as a downloadable extra         |
| Fast path to action          | Visitors mainly want to book, order, or find you                       | Put key CTAs in the header and repeat them lower on important pages                |

## Primary success metrics

- Table bookings completed

- Menu page views and engagement

- Private hire / events enquiry submissions

- Clicks for directions, phone, and opening hours

- Organic visibility for core local intent terms such as venue type + town, Sunday roast + town, private hire + town, and pub quiz + town

# 2. Business model assumptions and audience

Use this version as the base brief unless your venue model is very different.

| Venue type          | Restaurant, pub, gastropub, or hybrid food-and-drink concept                                                                                  |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Location model      | One physical venue with a real local catchment                                                                                                |
| Acquisition mix     | Google Search, Google Maps, direct traffic, Instagram/social, repeat guests, and branded searches                                             |
| User intents        | Book, browse menu, see the vibe, check opening times, find directions, ask about private hire, or confirm whether the venue suits an occasion |
| Commercial priority | Turn local demand into visits and bookings with minimal friction                                                                              |

## Core audience segments

| **Audience**          | **What they need from the site**                                                             | **What the page must prove**                                 |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Local diners/drinkers | Fast understanding of offer, vibe, and price point                                           | The venue matches the mood and occasion                      |
| Intent searchers      | A direct answer to terms like Sunday roast, brunch, private dining, quiz night, or cocktails | A dedicated page or clear homepage section covers that offer |
| Visitors / tourists   | Location, opening times, proximity to landmarks or station, and booking                      | The venue is easy to find and worth choosing                 |
| Group organisers      | Private hire details, room capacity, packages, and enquiry form                              | The venue can host the event professionally                  |
| Mobile walk-ins       | Directions, phone, current opening status, and menu                                          | The site is useful within seconds on a phone                 |

## Restaurant-led vs pub-led emphasis

The same structure works for both, but the emphasis should shift based on the revenue engine.

| **If more restaurant-led**                                                 | **If more pub-led**                                                                     |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Push menu quality, chef story, occasion-led dining, and booking confidence | Push atmosphere, events, live nights, sport, drinks selection, and spontaneous visiting |
| Use stronger food imagery and more menu detail above the fold              | Use stronger environment imagery and what’s-on messaging above the fold                 |
| Private dining is often a premium conversion path                          | Events, quizzes, functions, and seasonal campaigns often drive repeat traffic           |

# 3. Recommended site architecture

Keep the site small. A compact structure is easier to maintain, clearer for users, and stronger for local SEO than a bloated brochure site.

> **Recommended sitemap**
> Home / Menu / Book / Events-or-Private-Hire / Find-Us. Optional extras: About, Gift Cards, FAQs, Careers, News.

## Core pages and URL slugs

| **Page**              | **Suggested URL** | **Primary job**                                                   | **SEO intent**                                            |
|-----------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Home                  | /                 | Introduce the venue, trigger trust, and drive action              | venue type + main town/city                               |
| Menu                  | /menu             | Show food and drink clearly in live text                          | menu, food, drinks, roast, brunch + town                  |
| Book a Table          | /book             | Make reservation intent frictionless                              | brand + booking; supports conversion more than ranking    |
| Events / Private Hire | /events           | Sell group bookings, parties, regular events, and seasonal offers | private hire, party venue, pub quiz, live music + town    |
| Find Us               | /find-us          | Help people reach the venue easily                                | near landmark/station, directions, parking, accessibility |

## Navigation model

- Desktop header: logo, Home, Menu, Events/Private Hire, Find Us, Book CTA.

- Mobile header: logo + menu icon + sticky Book button or Book/Order split if both matter.

- Footer: opening hours, address, phone, email, booking link, socials, map, policies.

- Do not hide key actions inside multiple taps. Menu, booking, hours, and address must always be easy to reach.

## When to add more pages

- Add a dedicated Sunday Roast page if that offer materially drives demand.

- Add Brunch, Christmas, Weddings, or Gift Cards only when the offer is real and commercially important.

- Avoid thin nearby-area pages that say nearly the same thing with swapped place names.

# 4. Homepage blueprint

The homepage should answer the visitor’s main questions in about five seconds: what is this place, where is it, what does it feel like, and what should I do next?

## Recommended section order

| **Section**            | **Purpose**                       | **Required elements**                                                                                |
|------------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1\. Hero               | Instant positioning and first CTA | Venue name, category, town/neighbourhood, booking CTA, secondary menu CTA, opening status or hours   |
| 2\. Proof bar          | Fast trust and fit                | Rating, awards, key features such as dog-friendly, terrace, Sunday roast, craft beer, private dining |
| 3\. Why choose us      | Clarify the offer                 | 3 to 5 concise reasons the venue is worth choosing                                                   |
| 4\. Menu preview       | Drive intent deeper               | Signature dishes/drinks with prices or key categories and a full menu link                           |
| 5\. Atmosphere/gallery | Sell the experience               | Interior, exterior, bar, dining room, food, terrace, or social energy                                |
| 6\. Events / offers    | Promote recurring revenue drivers | Quiz, live music, happy hour, roast, tasting menu, match days, seasonal campaigns                    |
| 7\. Reviews            | Increase confidence               | Short review quotes or trust snippets                                                                |
| 8\. Visit block        | Convert local intent              | Map, address, parking/transit notes, accessibility, full opening times                               |
| 9\. Footer             | Complete the loop                 | Contact details, socials, booking, policies                                                          |

## Homepage hero formula

> **Template**
> Venue name
> Clear descriptor + location
> Primary CTA: Book a table
> Secondary CTA: View menu
> Support line: Open today until 11pm · 2 minutes from the station · Dog-friendly terrace

## Homepage content rules

- Use one strong promise, not vague lifestyle copy.

- Show the venue type and place name early in the headline or subheading.

- Use a single visual style. A premium restaurant tone and a lively local pub tone should not be mixed.

- Repeat the booking CTA before the lower half of the page.

- Show real information above the fold: hours, location cue, or a trust line.

# 5. Page-by-page blueprint

## Menu page

This page must be a genuine webpage, not only an uploaded PDF. A PDF can still be offered as a download, but the page itself should use live HTML text.

| **What to include**                                                            | **Why**                                                          |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Logical sections: food, drinks, desserts, Sunday roast, kids, brunch, specials | Helps users scan and helps search engines understand the content |
| Prices visible without zooming                                                 | Reduces friction and filters the right audience                  |
| Dietary markers and allergy note                                               | Improves usability and trust                                     |
| Prominent Book and Order CTAs                                                  | Captures intent while the user is deciding                       |
| Seasonal update process                                                        | Menus change often, so the CMS must make updates easy            |

## Book page

Keep this page extremely simple.

- Reservation widget or linked booking engine above the fold.

- Short reassurance text: walk-ins welcome if true, largest group size, same-day policy, and children/dogs/accessibility notes where relevant.

- Phone number and email fallback if the booking tool fails or the user has a special request.

## Events / Private Hire page

This page is often the best revenue-growth page after the homepage and menu.

| **Block**     | **Details to include**                                                                                             |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Positioning   | What the venue is suitable for: birthdays, work drinks, private dining, screenings, quiz nights, Christmas parties |
| Practical fit | Capacity, spaces available, indoor/outdoor split, standing vs seated, tech/equipment if relevant                   |
| Offer detail  | Packages, minimum spend, sample menus, or enquiry path                                                             |
| Social proof  | A quote, photo set, or simple trust note                                                                           |
| Enquiry path  | Short form and direct contact options                                                                              |

## Find Us page

This page closes the gap between search intent and physical arrival.

- Full business name, address, phone, email, map embed or linked map.

- Opening hours, nearest station/landmark, parking notes, and local direction hints.

- Accessibility details: step-free access, toilets, outdoor seating, dog policy, family suitability - only if accurate.

- This page should be especially strong on mobile.

## Optional About page

Only add this page if the venue has a story that genuinely supports brand value - for example a chef-led concept, local sourcing story, historic building, or premium positioning. If the story is weak, the homepage can carry the essentials instead.

# 6. Geo and SEO blueprint

For a hospitality website, local intent is the core search opportunity. The goal is to make the site unmistakably relevant for the main town or city, while also giving users and search engines clear signals about the venue’s real catchment and offer.

> **Main strategy**
> Own one primary place hard. Support it with a few genuine nearby modifiers, then build separate pages for real user intents such as menu, Sunday roast, private hire, brunch, or quiz night.

## Primary geo target

| Primary location target | Main town/city + optional neighbourhood                               |
|-------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Secondary modifiers     | Real nearby landmarks, station name, district, or city-centre wording |
| Do not do               | Thin duplicate pages for every nearby town or district                |

## Keyword map

| **Page**              | **Primary keyword pattern**      | **Support terms**                                                |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Home                  | \[venue type\] in \[town\]       | near \[landmark\], \[district\], city centre                     |
| Menu                  | menu / food / drinks in \[town\] | Sunday roast, cocktails, brunch, lunch, dinner                   |
| Events / Private Hire | private hire in \[town\]         | party venue, group dining, pub quiz, live music, Christmas party |
| Find Us               | \[brand\] \[town\]               | near station, parking, directions, accessibility                 |

## On-page SEO requirements

- Each important page needs a unique title tag, H1, and meta description.

- Use the town/city naturally in the title, H1, opening paragraph, and location section where relevant.

- Use internal links with sensible anchor text such as View menu, Book a table, or Private hire in Bath.

- Use short, readable URLs.

- Keep image filenames and alt text descriptive, but never stuffed with repeated keywords.

## Google Business Profile alignment

The website and the Google Business Profile should reinforce each other.

- Business name, address, phone, opening hours, booking link, and menu link should match the site.

- Choose the right primary category and use category-specific features such as reservations, menus, and online ordering where available.

- Upload strong recent venue photos and respond to reviews consistently.

## Structured data

Add LocalBusiness / Restaurant structured data, plus the core business details that are visible on the page. Include menu URL, hours, address, phone, and other applicable fields.

Important: structured data should reflect the visible page content and live business details.

# 7. Copy and content brief

The copy should sound confident and specific. Users do not need abstract brand slogans first; they need to know what the place is, where it is, and why it is worth choosing.

## Tone rules

- Be concrete before clever.

- Use hospitality language that matches the venue type and price point.

- Lead with what the customer gets, not internal brand language.

- Keep paragraphs short and skimmable.

## Homepage copy starter

| **Element**   | **Template**                                                                             |
|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Headline      | \[Descriptor\] \[venue type\] in \[town/neighbourhood\]                                  |
| Subheading    | \[Type of food/drink/experience\] for \[occasion or mood\], with \[key differentiator\]. |
| Proof bar     | 4.7★ rating / Sunday roasts / craft beer / terrace / private dining / dog-friendly       |
| Why choose us | 3 to 5 concise reasons, each under 10 words if possible                                  |
| Visit block   | Find us in \[area\]. \[Landmark or station cue\]. Open \[days/times\].                   |

## Photography brief

- Exterior that makes the venue easy to recognise on arrival.

- At least one wide interior image showing atmosphere and lighting.

- Bar, dining room, terrace/garden, and 4 to 8 signature food/drink images.

- Use real guests only if the photography feels authentic and release-safe.

- Avoid stock imagery.

## Assets to gather before build

| **Asset**                  | **Owner**          | **Notes**                                            |
|----------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Final logo and favicon     | Brand/owner        | Supply vector if available                           |
| Approved photography       | Photographer/owner | Crop for hero, card, square, and mobile ratios       |
| Current menus              | Operations/chef    | Confirm seasonal update process                      |
| Booking links and policies | Operations         | Cut-off times, walk-ins, group policy, children/dogs |
| Private hire details       | Sales/owner        | Capacity, packages, enquiry email, sample menus      |
| Legal pages                | Owner/legal        | Privacy, cookies, terms where needed                 |

# 8. Design and UX rules

The design should fit the venue. A polished site is not one with the most effects; it is one where tone, imagery, typography, and layout all support the same commercial message.

## Design direction

| **Dimension** | **Rule**                                                                    |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Visual tone   | Match the actual venue - premium, relaxed, lively, classic, or contemporary |
| Typography    | Use one display style and one body style; keep it highly readable on mobile |
| Colour        | Use a restrained palette rooted in the brand - one main accent is enough    |
| Imagery       | Use real venue photography; lead with mood and recognisable space           |
| Buttons       | One primary CTA style across the whole site                                 |

## UX rules

- Sticky booking CTA on mobile.

- Tap targets large enough for thumbs.

- Do not hide opening times or address in a footer only.

- Keep forms short and easy to complete on mobile.

- Show the menu in live text with jump links for long menus.

## Accessibility baseline

- Readable contrast, sensible font sizes, keyboard-friendly navigation, descriptive link text, and alt text for meaningful images.

- Do not communicate important information only via colour.

- If using PDFs for menus or brochures, make the main site content still accessible in HTML.

# 9. Technical build specification

The build should prioritise reliability, speed, and easy updates over novelty. The team must be able to change menus, opening hours, events, and seasonal blocks without rebuilding the site.

## Platform requirements

| **Area**     | **Requirement**                                                                     |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| CMS/editor   | Simple content editing for menus, events, homepage modules, and opening times       |
| Performance  | Fast image delivery, compressed assets, sensible script loading, and stable layout  |
| Mobile-first | Responsive layouts designed from mobile upward                                      |
| SEO          | Editable page titles, descriptions, canonicals, schema, XML sitemap, robots control |
| Analytics    | GA4 and Search Console at minimum; event tracking for key CTAs                      |
| Integrations | Booking engine, maps, optional ordering, optional email capture                     |
| Security     | HTTPS, cookie consent where needed, basic hardening and updates                     |

## Technical checklist for the developer

- Create clean semantic heading structure on every page.

- Use crawlable internal links and readable URLs.

- Optimise hero media for mobile performance.

- Avoid layouts that shift noticeably during load.

- Set up favicons, social sharing metadata, and preferred images.

- Submit XML sitemap in Search Console after launch.

## Analytics events to track

| **Event**                     | **Purpose**                                        |
|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| Book CTA clicks               | Understand booking demand by page                  |
| Menu PDF download, if offered | See whether users still need a downloadable format |
| Directions/map clicks         | Measure local visit intent                         |
| Phone taps                    | Measure urgent mobile contact intent               |
| Private hire form submissions | Track revenue-led enquiries                        |

# 10. Launch checklist and first 30 days

## Pre-launch checklist

- All pages reviewed on mobile and desktop.

- Opening hours, phone, address, booking link, menu, and map verified.

- Title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, and schema reviewed.

- Images compressed and alt text added where appropriate.

- Analytics, Search Console, cookie handling, and form notifications tested.

- Google Business Profile updated with the final website and menu links.

## First 30 days after launch

| **Week** | **Action**                                                                                       |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Week 1   | Check indexing, broken links, analytics events, and booking-path usability                       |
| Week 2   | Refine homepage proof points, key images, and event blocks based on behaviour                    |
| Week 3   | Publish or improve one high-intent supporting page such as Sunday Roast, Brunch, or Private Hire |
| Week 4   | Review search queries, local visibility, and Google Business Profile engagement                  |

## What success looks like

- Users can understand the venue and act within seconds.

- The site ranks or begins to rank for the venue type + town and offer + town searches that matter.

- The team can update key content without technical bottlenecks.

- The website, Google Business Profile, and real-world venue experience all match.
