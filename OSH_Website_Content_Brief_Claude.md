### About the Pub

*Source: Business Plan (Inn Control / Lapen Inns OSH Ltd)*

The Old School House — previously known as The Plough — is an impressive brick building on the busiest road in Stony Stratford. Attractively and traditionally decorated with exposed brick, wooden floors, and carpet, it stands out to all passing traffic.

The pub benefits from a large single-bar layout that reduces operational costs, 65 internal covers, 60 external covers, a commercial trade kitchen, an underground cellar, a small car park, and a private courtyard. The wonderful outdoor front area comes complete with wooden seating — a massive boost as it is so central and caters for near year-round trade.

**Key features (verbatim from business plan):**
- Large single bar layout reducing operational costs
- Underground cellar accessible from behind the bar
- 65 internal covers, 60 external covers
- Open plan bar space
- Commercial trade kitchen
- Small car park
- Private courtyard and storage
- Exposed brick, wooden floors, and carpet
- Front outdoor area with wooden seating
- Private accommodation: 2-bed self-contained flat with own access (operator use)

---

### About the Operator — Sanjog Gautam ('San')

*Source: Business Plan personal profile section*

Sanjog — known as San — is a vastly experienced business and restaurant manager, having worked in a range of businesses with great success. A dedicated and hard-working individual with a passion for hospitality, San brings a fresh approach to the Old School House, transplanting his successful business model from his current site into the larger outlet.

Having operated a café, restaurant, and a pub, San has developed a nuanced and varied range of abilities. He hopes to retain the classic pub ethos of the business while adding to it with his own brand of welcome, product, and service — including his well-loved Nepalese food offering.

---

### Part of the Lapen Inns Family

The Old School House is operated by Lapen Inns OSH Ltd, part of the Lapen Inns group of pubs across Cambridgeshire, Bedfordshire, and Norfolk — each blending Nepalese kitchens with classic pub comfort.

- Website: [lapeninns.com](https://lapeninns.com)
- Company: Lapen Inns OSH Ltd — Company No. 16699719

---

### Location

Stony Stratford is a busy, picturesque market town at the north-west corner of Milton Keynes in north Buckinghamshire. The pub is situated towards the top of a busy and bustling High Street (London Road). Pedestrians stream up and down it each day, and the road is a busy access route to the shopping centre, car parking, and office areas. Abundant public transport and parking are available throughout the area.

The highlight of the annual calendar is the **Folk on the Green** festival in early June (second Sunday), when folk music and folk rock takes over nearby Horsefair Green.

## Brand & Design Reference

*Sources: Website Blueprint §7–8 · Lapen Inns family sites*

---

### Tone & Copy Rules

- Be concrete before clever
- Lead with what the customer gets, not internal brand language
- Keep paragraphs short and skimmable
- Show venue type and place name early in headline or subheading
- Use one strong promise — not vague lifestyle copy
- Use a single visual style throughout (do not mix premium-restaurant tone with lively-local-pub tone)

---

### Design Direction

| Dimension | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Visual tone | Modern but traditional — exposed brick, wood, classic pub with Nepalese warmth |
| Typography | One display style + one body style; highly readable on mobile |
| Colour | Restrained palette rooted in the brand — one main accent. Dark navy + warm gold observed across Lapen Inns sites |
| Imagery | Real venue photography; lead with mood and recognisable space. Avoid stock. |
| Buttons | One primary CTA style across the whole site |

---

### Photography Brief

- Exterior: makes venue easy to recognise on arrival (prominent brick building on London Road)
- At least one wide interior showing atmosphere, exposed brick, wooden floors
- Bar, dining room, front garden / outdoor seating
- 4–8 signature food/drink images (Nepalese dishes + pub classics)
- Real guests only if photography feels authentic and release-safe
- Avoid stock imagery
